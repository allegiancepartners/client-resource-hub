Your company was incorporated in 2023, meaning your shares qualify for the Section 1202 QSBS exclusion in 2028 — two years before your target exit. The exclusion eliminates federal capital gains tax on up to $10M of gain per taxpayer. A Dynasty Trust, established and funded before that date, qualifies as a separate taxpayer under §1202(h)(2) and can access its own $10M exclusion. John and your T&E counsel will advise on the optimal approach.

After the exit, the Dynasty Trust has the option to deploy its proceeds into a Private Placement Life Insurance policy — either FCV (Frozen Cash Value) or ZCV (Zero Cash Value). Both produce permanently tax-exempt investment growth under current US law. Held within the Irrevocable Trust, the assets also sit completely outside your taxable estate — so when the time comes, the full compounded value passes to your beneficiaries free of both income tax and estate tax.
